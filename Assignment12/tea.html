About Tea Leaves

～ Type of Tea Leaves ～

• Black Tea •

Fully oxidized tea leaves, giving a dark color and rich flavor. Contains more caffeine than other teas.

• Green Tea •

Made from unoxidized leaves; high in antioxidants and polyphenols.

• White Tea •

Minimally processed young leaves. Holds a high amount of antioxidants.

• Yellow Tea •

A rare tea dried slowly for a yellow color and mellow green-tea-like flavor.

(image source)

• Oolong Tea •

Partially oxidized; flavors range from floral to deep and roasted.

• Pu-erh Tea •

A fermented tea with a rich, earthy taste. Often aged for complexity.

• Herbal Tea •

Herbal blends made from flowers, herbs, or fruits—not true teas.
