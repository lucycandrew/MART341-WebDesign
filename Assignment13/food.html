Food for a Tea Party

Tea parties typically feature a delicious assortment of finger sandwiches, scones, and delicate pastries served as small, bite-sized portions. The food is often presented on a tiered tray for an elegant touch.

～ Savory Selections ～

Cucumber Tea Sandwiches

Crisp English cucumber layered with herb-infused cream cheese made with dill, chives, lemon juice, and a hint of garlic.

Benedictine Spread

A Kentucky classic using cream cheese, cucumber, onion, and spices, served on soft white bread cut into triangles.

Egg Salad Sandwiches

A traditional tea party staple, often paired with watercress and served on thin white or wheat bread.

Smoked Salmon & Cream Cheese

An elegant option combining rich smoked salmon with a simple chive cream cheese spread.

Ham & Cheese Cucumber Bites

A heartier, low-carb option using cucumber slices instead of bread, secured neatly with a toothpick.

～ Classic Scones ～

Lavender & Earl Grey Scones

Light, buttery, and flaky scones infused with fragrant Earl Grey tea leaves and delicate culinary lavender, finished with a bright citrus glaze.

Cherry-Rose Scones

A unique pairing of dehydrated cherries and a subtle hint of rose water, ideal with black tea.

Classic Cream Scones

Traditional tea-time scones made with flour, butter, and cream, with optional currants or lemon peel. (Best with jam & butter or clotted cream .)

～ Sweet Treats ～

Mexican Widding Cakes (or Russian Tea Cakes)

Buttery, crumbly cookies dusted generously with powdered sugar. These melt-in-your-mouth treats are a classic addition to any tea table.

Lavender Scottish Shortbread

Traditional Scottish shortbread with a delicate hint of lavender, offering a fragrant, lightly sweet pairing for afternoon tea.

Poppy Seed Cake

A moist, tender cake dotted with poppy seeds and often finished with a light citrus glaze for brightness. (Lavender going well with everything.)

Signature Tea Cakes

Mini loaf cakes in flavors like chocolate swirl, lemon vanilla blueberry, and banana pecan, offering variety for every guest.

Fresh Fruit

A light and refreshing selection of seasonal fresh fruit such as berries, grapes, melon, and citrus slices. Fresh fruit balances the sweets and adds color and brightness to the tea table.
